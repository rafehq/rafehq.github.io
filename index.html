I am a Senior Research Specialist at CEHD, working with Professor James Heckman, Stephen Durlauf and Salvador Navarro. I got my Masterâ€™s in Econometrics from UChicago, advised by Professor Stephen Durlauf.

My primary research areas of interest are labor economics and econometrics, with a focus in identifying how skills interact with opportunities in the labor market to explain the heterogenous impacts of policy interventions. Several of my projects have focused on the Intergenerational Mobility, and its drivers in Denmark and the US. A major project has involved trying to map economic time series across generations to get a better sense of what creates mobility across generations, and create a measure that better takes into the broader functional relationships that would more holistically capture components of individual welfare, including notions such as credit constraints and uncertainty.
